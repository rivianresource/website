Rivian Delivery Checklist

Inspect before you sign and drive away. Tap each item to mark it done.

Most delivery issues are minor and easy to miss in the excitement of pickup. A systematic walk-around takes 30–60 minutes — documenting issues before you leave is far easier than reporting them later.

Tap each item to check it off. Photograph anything that looks off and file a service request in the Rivian app before you drive away.

This checklist reflects 2025–2026 R1T and R1S specifications to the best of our knowledge. Features and included items vary by trim and production date — always verify against your window sticker and order confirmation.

Exterior
Full walk-around in good light
0/16
Panel gaps consistent all around
Door-to-door, door-to-fender, hood-to-fender. Uneven gaps are a common delivery issue.
Important
Paint — no chips, scratches, or swirl marks
Inspect in daylight or under bright service center lights. Check all four corners and hood leading edge.
Important
Exterior trim and badging — no peeling or misalignment
Run your finger along all trim pieces. Check that Rivian lettering is straight and fully adhered.
Note
All doors open, close, and latch smoothly
Every door: frunk, tailgate (R1T), liftgate (R1S). Door alignment was an issue at my delivery.
Critical
Powered tonneau cover operates smoothly (R1T — if equipped)
Standard on Tri and Quad. A $2,000 option on Dual-motor models. Note: early Gen 2 Dual builds may still have manual slats — verify your window sticker matches what's on the vehicle. If equipped with the powered version, test open, close, and stop. Check the seal along all four edges.
Important
Headlights and taillights — no condensation or damage
Check for moisture inside the housing. Test DRL, low beam, and high beam function.
Important
Lightbar illuminates evenly — front and rear
Gen 2 horizontal lightbar should light end to end with no dim zones or flickering.
Note
Horn works
Quick test in the lot. Also confirms steering wheel controls are functional.
Note
Wheels — no curb rash, correct spec
Verify wheel size and color match your order. Check all four for transport damage.
Important
Tires — correct spec, no sidewall damage, proper pressure
Check sidewalls for bulging or cuts. Verify PSI. Check for uneven wear.
Important
Undercarriage — no shipping straps or transport blocks left on
Crouch and look under all four corners. Transport hardware occasionally gets left on during delivery.
Critical
Trailer hitch cover present and properly seated
The integrated 2" hitch receiver is standard on all R1T and R1S models. Verify the cover panel is installed — it's aerodynamic and easy to lose during transport.
Note
Gear Tunnel doors align flush and latch easily (R1T)
A known delivery issue: R1T Gear Tunnel doors occasionally require significant force to latch or sit proud of the body. Open and close both sides and check for flush fit — misalignment is much easier to document on-site than after.
Important
Frunk — opens, closes, latches, seal intact
Check the rubber seal around the perimeter. Water intrusion into the frunk has been reported on some units.
Important
Charge port opens, closes, and accepts a cable
Test manually and via the Rivian app. Try plugging in a cable if available. NACS port is standard on all 2026 models.
Critical
Cameras — clean lenses, no protective film left on
Check all Gear Guard cameras. Verify they show a clean image in the app.
Note
Glass & Wipers
Windshield, windows, wipers
0/4
Windshield — no chips, cracks, or delamination
Inspect at an angle to catch chips and star cracks. Check edges for delamination (white haze or bubbling at the perimeter).
Critical
Side and rear glass — no chips, cracks, or scratches
Run your eye along each pane. Check the liftgate glass on R1S carefully.
Important
Wiper blades — no streaking, correct fitment
Run wipers at all speeds with washer fluid spray. Check for streaking or skipping. Rain-sensing wipers are standard — verify auto mode activates.
Note
Washer fluid reservoir has fluid
Spray front and rear washers. Low or empty fluid on a new vehicle is not uncommon.
Note
Interior
Trim, seats, materials, and storage
0/17
Wood / trim panels — no defects, gaps, or misalignment
A defect in my Dark Ash Wood trim was caught at delivery. Run your hand along all trim edges.
Important
Seats — no tears, stains, or stitching defects
Check all rows, especially seat bolsters — a common spot for cosmetic defects.
Important
Seat adjustments — all powered seats move in every direction
Test every direction on all adjustable seats. Check memory seat positions if equipped. Eight-way power-adjustable front seats are standard.
Important
Seat belts — retract and latch on all rows
Active recall on some 2022–2026 R1S/R1T for improperly tightened seat belt retractor bolts. Confirm with the service advisor that this has been addressed on your unit before delivery.
Critical
Glass roof — operates correctly
All trims include a panoramic fixed glass roof. If you have the Sound + Vision package (Dual trim) or Tri/Quad trim, you'll have the dynamic electronically reconfigurable glass — the transition is binary (opaque vs. transparent), not graduated. Verify it switches fully between both states with no stuck zones or uneven patches.
Important
All windows — operate smoothly without grinding or rattling
Test every window. Check for slow operation or noise in the door channels.
Note
USB-C ports — test all with your device
Bring a cable. Eight USB-C ports are standard — verify front console, rear seat, and any other ports charge. Dead ports are easy to miss.
Important
Wireless charging pad working
Place your phone on the pad and confirm it charges. Check surface for scratches.
Note
Center console — no gaps, pass-through slot functional
Test the pass-through slot and all storage areas. Confirm the console lid doesn't bind.
Note
No unusual odors
Mold, chemical, or musty smells can indicate water intrusion during transport. Trust your nose.
Important
Rivian Torch present in driver's side door — powers on
A 1,000-lumen rechargeable flashlight is standard on all R1T and R1S models, stowed in the driver's side door frame where it charges automatically. Press to eject and test all three brightness settings. It's a frequently missing or stolen item — verify it's there before leaving.
Important
Second and third row seats — no rattles or clicking
Physically shake each rear seat while stationary. A known issue on some R1S units is a clicking or tapping sound from the B-pillar area or second-row seat latches. Easier to document and flag at delivery than after.
Important
Headliner — no sagging, smudges, or witness marks
Check the headliner near the A, B, and C pillars for fingerprints or grease marks left during assembly or detailing. Also check for any sagging along the edges.
Note
Spare tire kit present (if ordered)
The All-Terrain Package includes a compact spare. If you ordered it, verify it's installed in the correct location with all hardware.
Note
First Aid Kit present
A basic first aid kit is standard on R1T (stowed in the right gear tunnel door) and R1S (under the cargo subfloor). Verify it's there — it's a commonly missing item on used vehicles and occasionally absent at new delivery.
Note
Air Compressor Accessory Kit present
Standard on all 2026 R1T and R1S models. Verify the hose and adapters are present — stowed in the gear tunnel door on R1T, cargo subfloor on R1S. A 20-foot hose extends to all four wheels and includes adapters for tires, bicycle tubes, and inflatable gear.
Important
Front license plate bracket present (2-plate states)
If you live in a state requiring a front plate, verify the bracket is in the vehicle — typically in the frunk or gear tunnel. Easier to get one before you leave than to chase it down later.
Note
Tech & Software
Screens, connectivity, keys, and app pairing
0/9
Main touchscreen — no dead zones, artifacts, or backlight bleed
Tap all corners and edges of the 15.6" screen. Look for unresponsive areas or display issues.
Critical
Driver display — functioning and accurate
Verify speed, range, and navigation display correctly on the 12.3" digital cluster. Check for pixel issues or dead spots.
Important
Rear touchscreen working (R1S)
The R1S includes a 6.8" rear console touchscreen for rear passengers. Verify it responds and functions correctly.
Note
Rivian app paired and connected
Verify the vehicle appears in your app. Test remote lock/unlock and horn honk from the app before leaving.
Critical
Software on current version
Check Settings → About. If significantly behind, ask the service center to update before you leave.
Important
Bluetooth and phone key working
Test phone key unlock and start. Pair a second phone if you have a co-driver. Confirm Bluetooth audio connects.
Important
KeyCards present (2) — test NFC unlock on both readers
Gen 2 vehicles (2025+) do not include a key fob — you receive two KeyCards and mobile app access. Test each card on BOTH the B-pillar exterior reader and the internal center console reader. Some units have arrived with faulty internal readers. Key fobs are a separate Gear Shop purchase.
Important
All speakers working — no distortion
Play music at moderate volume and check each zone. If you have the Sound + Vision package or Tri/Quad trim: test the Rivian Premium Audio at higher volume for any distortion.
Note
Gear Guard enabled and cameras visible in live view
If you brought a USB-C SSD, set up Gear Guard storage during delivery. Verify all camera feeds are clear.
Note
HVAC & Climate
Heating, cooling, and airflow
0/7
Heat produces warm air within a few minutes
An issue at my delivery — heater took 20+ minutes. Set to max heat and time it. Should produce warm air within 3–5 minutes.
Critical
A/C cools properly from all vents
Run A/C on max for a few minutes. Verify cold air from front and rear vents.
Important
Steering wheel centered when wheels are straight
Many owners report the steering wheel arriving 2–5 degrees off-center. With wheels pointed straight ahead, the wheel spokes should be perfectly level. Flag it now — correction requires a wheel alignment adjustment at the service center.
Important
All vents direct airflow correctly
Test each zone including rear rows. Dual-zone automatic climate control is standard. Check that rear vents respond to climate controls.
Note
Front seat heating works on all levels
Heated front seats are standard on all trims. Test all heat levels on both driver and passenger seats.
Note
Front seat ventilation (cooling) works on all levels
Ventilated/cooled front seats are standard on all trims. Test all cooling levels on both seats — a separate function from heating, usually found in the climate menu.
Note
Rear seat heating works (all equipped rows)
Heated rear seats are standard on all trims. R1S has heated seats in rows 2 and 3. R1T has heated rear seats. Test all levels.
Note
Charging & Range
Battery, range, and charging setup
0/4
Battery state of charge matches expected delivery level
Most deliveries are at 50–80%. Verify the displayed range is reasonable for your battery configuration.
Important
Portable Charger present (only if ordered separately)
As of 2026, the Portable Charger is no longer included — it's a $400 add-on. If you ordered one, verify it's in the vehicle and undamaged. If you didn't, plan your home charging setup before taking delivery.
Important
Charging schedule configured in app
Set up your home charging schedule before leaving. Configure off-peak hours if your utility offers TOU rates.
Note
Referral rewards — points and free charging (check within 2 weeks)
If you used a referral code, neither the Gear Shop points nor the 3 months of free Adventure Network charging will appear at delivery. Both are credited to your account within 2 weeks of delivery. Check under Rewards in your Rivian account — contact support if missing after that.
Note
Documentation
Paperwork and records
0/4
Window sticker / Monroney label saved
Photograph it or save via Rivian Roamer VIN lookup. Useful for warranty and insurance purposes.
Note
Any pre-existing issues photographed and logged in app
Do this before leaving. Documentation at delivery carries far more weight than claims filed after the fact.
Critical
Service request filed for any noted issues
Open a service request in the Rivian app before leaving. The delivery tech can often add notes on the spot.
Critical
VIN recorded
Note your VIN for insurance and recall lookups. Found on the driver door jamb and the dashboard (visible through the windshield).
Note
✓ Inspection Complete
All 61 items checked. File any open service requests before you drive away — and enjoy the truck.

From my delivery: Using a checklist caught three issues I would have missed — two misaligned doors, a defect in the Dark Ash Wood trim, and an HVAC problem where the heater took over 20 minutes to produce warm air. Rivian's app-based service request system made documenting everything fast and straightforward.

If you find issues, don't panic. Rivian expects some and the process for getting them fixed is clear. The key is documenting everything before you drive away.
