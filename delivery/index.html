Rivian Delivery Checklist

What to inspect before you sign and drive away. Based on real owner delivery experience — the issues most commonly missed at pickup.

Most Rivian delivery issues are minor and easy to miss in the excitement of pickup. A systematic walk-around takes at least 30 minutes and is worth every second — getting issues documented before you leave the service center is significantly easier than reporting them after delivery.

Use this checklist on your phone during delivery. Tap each item to mark it checked. If you find something, note it in the Rivian app immediately and photograph everything.

Panel gaps consistent all around
Check door-to-door, door-to-fender, and hood-to-fender gaps. Should be uniform — uneven gaps are common.
Important
Paint — no chips, scratches, or swirl marks
Inspect in daylight or under bright service center lights. Check all four corners and the hood leading edge.
Important
All doors open, close, and latch smoothly
Test every door including the frunk, tailgate (R1T), and liftgate (R1S). Door alignment was an issue at my delivery.
Critical
Headlights and taillights — no condensation or damage
Check for moisture inside the housing. Turn them on to verify function.
Important
Lightbar functions and RGB works
Gen 2 horizontal lightbar should illuminate evenly. Check both front and rear.
Note
Wheels — no curb rash, correct spec
Verify wheel size and color match your order. Check for transport damage.
Important
Tires — correct spec, no damage, adequate pressure
Check the sidewalls for any bulging or cuts. Verify pressure is at recommended PSI.
Important
Frunk — opens, closes, latches correctly
Check the seal around the perimeter. Water intrusion into the frunk is a reported issue on some units.
Important
Charge port opens and closes smoothly
Test the charge port door manually and via the app. Try plugging in a cable if one is available.
Critical
Cameras — clean lenses, no obstructions
Check all Gear Guard cameras for protective film left on. Verify they appear in the app.
Note
Air suspension — raises and lowers on all four corners
Cycle through suspension heights in the vehicle settings. Watch for any corner that's slow to respond.

Interior
Trim, seats, and materials
0/9
Wood / trim panels — no defects, gaps, or misalignment
A defect in my Dark Ash Wood trim was caught at delivery using the inspection checklist. Run your hand along all trim edges.
Important
Seats — no tears, stains, or stitching issues
Check all rows. Look at the seat bolsters closely — these are a common spot for cosmetic defects.
Important
Seat adjustments work (all powered seats)
Test every direction on all adjustable seats. Check memory seat positions if equipped.
Important
Seat belts — retract and latch properly on all rows
Note: there is an active recall on some 2022–2025/2026 R1S and R1T for improperly tightened seat belt retractor bolts. Verify this has been addressed.
Critical
Dynamic glass roof — tints and clears correctly
Test full tint, partial tint, and clear mode. Verify the transition is even across the entire panel.
Note
All windows operate correctly

Note
Center console — no gaps, storage closes properly
Check the wireless charging area for damage. Test the pass-through slot and USB ports.
Note
No unusual odors

From my delivery: Using a checklist caught three issues I would have missed — two misaligned doors, a defect in the Dark Ash Wood trim, and an HVAC problem where the heater took over 20 minutes to produce warm air. Rivian's app-based service request system made documenting everything fast and straightforward. The service experience was genuinely good.

If you find issues, don't panic — Rivian expects some and the process for getting them fixed is clear. The key is documenting everything before you drive away.
