Owner perspective

I own the R1S Dual Max and the honest answer is: most buyers should seriously consider the R2 first. The price gap is significant — you could buy an R2 Performance and have $30,000+ left over. For the majority of daily driving situations, 335 miles of range is plenty, 5 seats is enough, and the R2's off-road capability is genuinely impressive even without locking differentials.

The R1S earns its premium if you actually use the third row, regularly tow, or do serious overlanding where ground clearance and water fording matter. The air suspension, 43-inch water fording depth, and locking differentials are real advantages in the right situations — not marketing.

The deciding question is usually seating, not performance. If you have kids in car seats and regular passengers filling all three rows, the R1S is the clear choice. If your household is 2–4 people, the R2 is probably the smarter buy.
