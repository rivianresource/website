Taking Delivery

Delivery from the Las Vegas service center was smooth overall. Before pickup, I used a Rivian delivery inspection checklist that helped me go through the vehicle more carefully than I probably would have on my own.

I highly recommend using one during delivery:

FREE Rivian Delivery Checklist

Because of that checklist, I noticed a few issues immediately including two doors needing alignment, a defect in part of the wood trim, and a heater issue where the cabin would not warm properly.

Rivian made the service request process very easy through the app. While I obviously would have preferred a perfect delivery, I appreciated having a clear process and support system for getting the issues documented immediately.

Service Experience

I visited the Rivian service center in Las Vegas, NV for the initial repairs.

Upon drop-off, one of the technicians came out to take a quick look at the vehicle with me. Honestly, he was incredibly friendly and helpful, which is pretty much the opposite of the stereotypical experience I've had dealing with auto techs in the past.

I was able to schedule the service appointment within about a week. A Rivian rep contacted me a few days before to confirm the issues and service appt date/time.

The door alignment and trim repairs were resolved fairly quickly, but because of the HVAC issues, Rivian ended up keeping the vehicle for a total of six days.

The heater situation has still been somewhat inconsistent. Sometimes it works perfectly. Other times it can take close to twenty minutes before warm air really starts coming through the vents. I'd estimate it behaves correctly about sixty percent of the time right now.

Rivian is sending a mobile technician to my house next week to continue troubleshooting. I've also noticed a slight rattle from the front passenger door. It becomes most noticeable during bass-heavy songs or on roads that are less than perfectly smooth.

Even with those issues, the overall service experience itself has been straightforward so far.

Charging

There's a Rivian charging station about fifteen minutes from my house, so I've taken advantage of the included three months of free charging several times.

The Las Vegas Rivian Adventure Network charging station is located at Town Square Shopping Mall:

6605 S Las Vegas Blvd, Las Vegas, NV 89119

Every time I visited, there were open chargers available even during late morning or afternoon hours.

Most of my charging happens at home though. Our house is on NV Energy's EV Time of Use plan, so we only charge our vehicles between midnight and 8am to keep charging costs as low as possible.

I use a Tesla Wall Charger on Level 2 charging and typically cap the battery around seventy percent for daily use.

I haven't taken the vehicle on a true road trip yet, so I can't fully speak to the long-distance charging experience. Still, having the native NACS port is a major advantage. Being able to use Tesla Superchargers without an adapter makes the ownership experience feel much less stressful.

I do feel slightly guilty taking up extra space at some Supercharger stations though.

Driving Experience

The driving experience is easily the highlight of the vehicle.

It's incredibly smooth, quiet, and refined. Occasionally I hear the air suspension compressor or the HVAC system working aggressively during heavy cooling or heating, but thankfully it has not been nearly as intrusive as some examples I've seen online.

I really enjoy the auto-kneel function when parking. I did disable automatic ride height adjustments while driving because I wanted to reduce how often the suspension constantly raises and lowers itself.

Oddly enough, every so often the setting seems to re-enable itself after getting back in the vehicle. I've already tried both soft and hard resets without consistent success.

Another strange software behavior involves the middle-row climate vents. Sometimes the middle-row airflow is active when the car starts, other times it is not. Since I have children in car seats, I find myself manually checking the rear airflow regularly.

Software quirks aside, I absolutely love driving this thing.

The acceleration with the Performance Upgrade is absurdly quick while still remaining smooth and controlled. The turning radius is surprisingly tight for a vehicle this size, and I've become very comfortable with the height and proportions of the R1S.

Sound & Vision Package

This may be controversial, but I think the updated 2026 Sound & Vision package sounds significantly better than many reviews suggest.

SiriusXM audio quality is average at best, but Apple Music with Dolby Atmos tracks sounds much better than expected.

Albums like Pink Floyd's Dark Side of the Moon, Fleetwood Mac's Rumours, John Coltrane's Blue Train, and Illenium's Odyssey all sound fantastic in the cabin.

Some albums definitely sound flatter than others, but that seems to come down more to the mix itself than the system.

I do think Rivian could push more power to the subwoofer in future software updates. Certain songs feel like they're missing that final layer of low-end punch.

The dynamic glass roof is cool too. It borders on gimmicky, but in Las Vegas heat I'll gladly take anything that helps reduce cabin temperatures. Still, I find myself activating it on sunny hot days and opening it back up the rest of the time. It's a fun feature and honestly everyone who rides in the vehicle seems impressed by it.

Tires and Wheel Choice

Tire wear is still too early to judge, but I'm watching it closely and fully expect these tires to wear faster than traditional SUV tires.

At the moment I still feel good about choosing the 22-inch Sport wheels.

When my vehicle was in service, Rivian loaned me a Launch Edition R1T equipped with all-terrain tires and the Meridian audio system.

Honestly, I thought the audio system in my R1S sounded noticeably better.

The all-terrain tires were also much louder than I expected. The truck had around 40,000 miles on it, so tire age may have played a role, but the road noise was distracting enough that it reinforced my decision to stay with the Sport setup.

I still think the all-terrain setup looks better visually on the R1S, but since about eighty-five percent of my driving is on pavement, I'd rather prioritize ride comfort and road noise.

Thankfully, the 22-inch Sport Dark wheels still look excellent.

Do I Miss ICE Vehicles?

Not at all.

My previous vehicles included a Jeep Grand Cherokee 4xe and a Land Rover Discovery. Both had their own issues, especially the Jeep.

At this point I no longer expect any modern vehicle to be perfect. That includes EVs.

But if the long-term driving experience remains this enjoyable, the R1S will very likely end up being the best vehicle I've ever owned.

Final Thoughts

Even with a few software quirks and some early service visits, the R1S has still been one of the most enjoyable vehicles I've owned.

Every time I walk up to it or get behind the wheel, it still feels special. The combination of acceleration, ride comfort, practicality, technology, and overall driving character makes it hard to go back to traditional SUVs.

I'm especially curious to see how the ownership experience evolves over the next year as Rivian continues refining the software platform and expanding charging access.

For now, the positives overwhelmingly outweigh the negatives.
