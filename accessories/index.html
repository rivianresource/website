Gear Guard Accessory

Samsung T7 Shield 1TB

Every Rivian owner eventually needs a drive for Gear Guard footage storage, and this is the one I landed on for a couple reasons.

First, it’s overwhelmingly the favorite recommendation across Rivian forums and Reddit discussions. After seeing it mentioned repeatedly by owners, it seemed like the safest choice.

Second, I specifically chose the Shield version because of its heat resistance. Las Vegas summers are brutal, and it made sense to use a drive that would handle extremely hot cabin temperatures better when parked outside in direct sun.

I went with the 1TB version mainly so the system overwrites footage less frequently. Maybe that’s overly cautious, but I feel like constant rewriting probably contributes to drives failing sooner over time.

I also like how compact and lightweight it is. I do wish Rivian had positioned the elastic retaining band directly below the USB-C ports, but with the 90-degree USB-C adapters, the cable routes pretty cleanly around the edge of the console to the rear storage area where the drive sits.

I used these: 90 Degree USB-C Adapters

Setup was easy. Rivian recognized the drive almost immediately after plugging it in and accepted the storage without any issues.

How to Setup Gear Guard Storage:

1. Plug the SSD into one of the Rivian USB-C data ports inside the center console.

2. Open:
Controls → Gear Guard → Gear Guard Storage

3. Format the drive through the Rivian interface.

4. Once formatting completes, Gear Guard footage and Drive Cam recordings will automatically begin saving to the SSD.

After setup, everything has worked flawlessly so far.

Verdict:

Easy recommendation for Rivian owners, especially those who live in areas with extreme temperatures.

Charging Accessory

A2Z CCS1 to NACS Adapter

First impressions:

The adapter arrived nicely packaged and includes a solid storage case. The adapter itself feels heavy, durable, and well made — definitely not cheap feeling.

Real-world usage:

For Rivian owners with a NACS port, a CCS1 to NACS adapter is pretty much essential right now. I’ve already used this several times at my local Rivian Adventure Network stations since all of them are currently set up as CCS1.

I keep it stored in the frunk so it’s always easy to grab when needed. Usage is straightforward. From what I understand, the adapter should be connected to the charging cable first before plugging it into the vehicle.

Every time I’ve used it so far, the Rivian recognized the charger immediately and charging started right away without any issues.

I decided to go with the A2Z version because of the strong feedback from other Rivian owners, and it’s also a bit less expensive than the Rivian-branded adapter, which currently retails around $200.

One thing worth mentioning: if an adapter causes damage to the vehicle, Rivian states that damage would not be covered under warranty. Personally, I view that as a pretty unlikely scenario, but it’s still important information to share.

Verdict:

Highly recommend.

Charging Accessory

TWRAPS Dual Wireless Phone Charger

I personally think this is one of the better additions I’ve made to the car so far.

I went with the Dark Ash Wood skin variant since it’s a pretty close match to the factory wood trim inside the Rivian. Once installed, it honestly looks like something Rivian should have included from the factory.

Before this, I constantly had issues with my phone sliding forward and moving off the stock charging area. I’d notice charging repeatedly disconnecting and reconnecting whenever I pushed the phone back into place. Even though Rivian added a divider in the middle of the charging pad, I found it pretty useless.

Charging on the factory pad is also slow enough that I understand why a lot of Rivian owners eventually just switch to using charging cables instead. Fortunately Rivian at least added the cable pass-through slot into the center console.

Enter the TWRAPS charger.

The biggest improvement is MagSafe compatibility. The phone actually stays in place and consistently charges during drives. I do still see a “Slow Charging” notification on the phone occasionally, but the important thing is the battery percentage is actually higher after driving instead of lower.

It genuinely works well.

The only issue I ran into was the charger itself occasionally lifting slightly when removing the phone. I fixed this pretty easily using a very small amount of thin 3M adhesive along the front and side edges. It holds securely now without needing anything permanent or aggressive.

Overall, I love it. The cables stay hidden inside the console, it looks factory installed, and it functions dramatically better than the stock charging pad.

Pro Tip:

I’d highly recommend picking up some 90-degree USB-C adapters for the center console so the cables don’t stick out awkwardly into the storage area.
I bought these: 90 Degree USB-C Adapters

Verdict:

5 Stars. Huge improvement over the factory setup.
